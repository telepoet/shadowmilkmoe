How Do I Catch Up?

Given the Cookie Run franchise's live-service update model, there remains a constant output of new content for recurrent players to enjoy pretty much every month - though this backlog of what's now five years worth of content can become quickly overwhelming to a newcomer. Be it you're fresh to Kingdom's storyline and want the full package, are looking to refresh yourself on key events ahead of Act 2, or are simply here because your friends are constantly raving that weird blue clown and you want to know what the fuss is about, proposed below is 3 solutions depending on the total amount of time and interest you have available to invest into catching up on CRK's plot.

Want the full package?

Absorbing the full story front-to-back is a pretty significant time investment spanning a couple dozen hours - possibly hundreds - especially if you're actually playing the game yourself, but if you have the time to spare (and are willing to meet the early story where its at) having that full picture naturally makes for the most fulfilling narrative experience. CRK is notoroiously bad at organizing its plot-relevant side-story content in a clear linear manner, so I've outlined a recommended watch order based on chronological release, for your convenience:

For ease of access, I've reflected this watch order in a YouTube playlist comprised of English dub game footage from the lovely Royal Mike.And one video from Isaiah Cordera, since RM apparently never uploaded footage of the Tower of Sweet Chaos after the English audio release? This list is not definitive, and in fact skips over several side stories that are not as immediately plot relevant (sorry tri-cone cup), but it should be enough to get you a majority of the picture while skipping over some unnecessary fluff. If you're curious about some of said additional stories, feel free to peruse the likes of the Cookie Run Wiki or the rest of Royal Mike's channel for an archive of pretty much every event in the game.

Want to know who Shadow Milk Cookie is?

For a Beast-focused fast track, I'd watch the game's Prologue, Crispia 7-10, at least some of Cookie Odyssey Chapter 1, and only then jump all the way to Beast-Yeast 1 and 2 once you have a clear outline of who Pure Vanilla Cookie is (as their stories are fairly intertwined). If you're only looking for clown-relevant content, then you'd watch Beast-Yeast 7 and 8 after that. I would not recommend progressing past that point without going back to previous content - since the finale naturally remains pretty dependent on knowledge of literally everyone else in the cast - but this should also serve as a pretty good sampler of the "high notes" of Arc 1's writing and help you decide if you want to invest further energy into the plot outside of knowing who this Strange Blue Creature is.

Want just the footnotes?

I'd skim the Cookie Run Wiki's World Exploration transcripts and then supplement with character coverage pages as needed whenever you meet a new cookie you're curious about. This of course takes out pretty much all of the fun of hearing the voice performances and soundtrack,Both of which I consider a pretty crucial part of my own emotional investment into the series, since the amount at which this game's voice talent takes their dialogue SO seriously unironically elevates the experience tenfold. At the game's best you get some genuinely phenomenal character performances, and at it's worst it makes the viewing experience unbelievably fucking funny. but if you really don't have any time to spare, the synopsis-writing volunteers on the wiki do a pretty good job hitting all the key points you'd need to understand what your friends are talking about during a discussion of the game's plot. As far as gameplay is concerned, meanwhile... good luck with that.
